How we use these sources

When you submit a query, AI-Medikelizar searches across the above databases using semantic retrieval to identify the most relevant and authoritative content. Our AI then synthesises a response grounded exclusively in the retrieved evidence — never from its internal training data alone.

Every claim in an answer is linked to at least one source citation. You can expand any citation to view the full reference details and access the original publication. We are transparent about the evidence behind every answer.

About AI-Medikelizar

Our mission

AI-Medikelizar exists to make evidence-based medical knowledge more accessible to clinicians, researchers, and informed patients. We believe that access to reliable, cited health information is a cornerstone of informed decision-making.

The tool is designed as a clinical reference — not a diagnostic aid, not a treatment planner, and never a substitute for professional judgement. Every answer points back to the source it came from, so you can verify, read in context, and draw your own conclusions.

The trust model

Source-restricted retrieval

Our retrieval system searches only the pre-indexed trusted databases listed on our Sources page. We do not draw from unfiltered web content, forums, or unverified repositories.

Citation-anchored generation

The AI model is instructed to compose answers using only the retrieved source text. Every factual statement is tied to a citation marker [1], [2] referencing a specific source card.

Human-reviewable evidence

Every source card includes the publication title, authors, journal or publisher, date, and a direct link to the original. You can always read the source for yourself.

Transparent limitations

We clearly communicate confidence levels, date ranges of indexed content, and gaps in coverage. No answer is presented as definitive — medicine is an evolving field.

Technical approach

AI-Medikelizar uses a retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipeline. When you ask a question, embeddings of your query are used to semantically search a curated corpus of medical literature. The most relevant passages are retrieved and passed to a large language model, which is instructed to:

  • Answer only from the provided source text
  • Mark every claim with an inline citation to its source passage
  • Explicitly state when the retrieved evidence is insufficient to answer
  • Never fabricate references or infer beyond what the sources say

Intended use

This tool is designed for:

  • Healthcare professionals seeking a quick synthesis of current evidence on a specific clinical question
  • Researchers exploring a new topic area and looking for key papers
  • Patients and caregivers who want to better understand a condition or treatment from authoritative sources — in consultation with their healthcare team

It is not designed for emergency medical questions, diagnostic decision-making, or as a replacement for clinical judgement.
